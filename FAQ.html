Frequently Asked Questions

Fighting Fake News is providing extensive coverage and information about fake news and answering common questions

How is this different from “fake news”?

This work explores both false and true information and their spread. According to a recent Gallup Poll, Americans refer to three distinct meanings when using the phrase “fake news”:

  • False news presented as truth
  • Opinion presented as fact
  • True news that cast a politician or political party in a negative light

Our study was focused on contested news which often ends up being addressed by fact-checking organizations, and corresponds to (1) and (2) but not type (3) of “fake news.”

How do you determine which sites contain real news and which are false?

The stories contained in the sites have already been investigated by some or all of the six independent fact-checking organizations. The veracity of these stories are confirmed and extracted from the organizations’ websites and used as ground truth in our analysis.
